## Background

comdirect, a brand of Commerzbank AG, operates one of Germany's largest retail banking customer service organisations. With hundreds of thousands of customer interactions per year across email, phone, and chat, maintaining accurate and up-to-date documentation for frontline teams is a constant operational challenge.

## The Challenge

comdirect's customer service teams relied on Sabio, their internal knowledge base, to guide responses to customer inquiries. But the documentation hadn't kept pace with operational reality. Analysis revealed that roughly 50% of incoming support cases had no documented solution, meaning agents were solving problems from memory or through time-consuming manual research.

Marvin's team, responsible for knowledge quality, was spending 15–30 minutes per case simply locating relevant information and verifying that existing articles were still accurate. Documentation updates were reactive, slow, and dependent on individual experts flagging issues manually.

## How Interloom Helped

Interloom built a pipeline to process comdirect's full email dataset, 2.5 million threads covering 2024 and 2025, extracting problem-solution pairs from unstructured customer conversations.

Each extracted case was automatically compared against existing Sabio documentation. The system flagged four categories: cases with documented solutions, cases with no documentation, cases where the agent response contradicted the official article, and cases that were irrelevant to documentation.

Expert teams received a prioritised review queue. Where Interloom identified a gap, it generated a draft documentation suggestion. Marvin's team reviewed, edited where needed, and published directly to Sabio, reducing the per-case workload from 30 minutes to 5–10 minutes.

A real-time dashboard visualised the full problem landscape: 500+ identified subcategories, clustered by semantic similarity, with colour-coded documentation status across the entire corpus. New daily batches of 4,000–5,000 emails are now processed continuously, surfacing emerging issues within 15 minutes.

## Results

Over 80% of Interloom's documentation suggestions were accepted by comdirect's expert teams with minimal or no editing. The undocumented case rate dropped from 50% to under 5% following the initial cleanup. Daily operations now surface an average of 265 actionable documentation gaps, allowing the knowledge team to stay ahead of emerging issues rather than responding after escalations occur.
