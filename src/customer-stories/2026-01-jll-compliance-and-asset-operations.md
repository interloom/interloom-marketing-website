## Background

JLL is a global leader in real estate services and investment management, operating across 80+ countries. Its internal teams manage everything from property compliance inspections to capital markets debt processing, each with distinct regulatory and operational requirements.

## The Challenge

Each business line — Integral facility management, compliance engineering, capital markets, and asset operations — operated its own set of tools, spreadsheets, and manual coordination patterns. Compliance mapping alone required reconciling data from five different systems, and there was no shared view of workflow state across teams.

When audit requests came in, teams spent days assembling evidence from fragmented sources. Process changes in one area had no visibility to adjacent teams, leading to duplicated work and inconsistent outcomes.

## How Interloom Helped

JLL's compliance engineering team was the first to deploy Interloom, building an inbox-to-work-order workflow that replaced a manual triage process. The success led to rapid adoption across three additional business lines.

Capital markets teams now process debt quotes through structured workflow stages, with AI-assisted extraction reducing manual data entry. Asset operations uses predictive maintenance workflows that combine sensor data context with expert review steps. Across all four areas, every action and decision is traceable back to its source.

Domain experts in each business line own their workflow definitions and iterate independently, while governance teams maintain visibility across the full portfolio.

## Results

Compliance mapping cycles are 67% faster. Four business lines now run production workflows on a single platform, and manual data reconciliation across systems dropped by 85%. Audit preparation that previously took days now takes hours.
