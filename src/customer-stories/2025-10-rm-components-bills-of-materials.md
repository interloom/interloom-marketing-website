## Background

RM Components is a precision engineering firm that manufactures complex assemblies for industrial clients. Every production run begins with a bill of materials — a detailed specification of components, quantities, suppliers, and tolerances that must be accurate before manufacturing can start.

## The Challenge

BOM processing was the bottleneck in every production cycle. Engineers manually extracted component data from supplier PDFs, cross-referenced it against inventory records, checked tolerances against design specifications, and flagged discrepancies for purchasing. A single complex BOM could take half a day to process, and errors caught late in the cycle caused costly production delays.

## How Interloom Helped

RM Components built a BOM processing workflow in Interloom that covers the full cycle — from supplier document intake through component extraction, inventory matching, tolerance validation, and exception routing.

AI-assisted extraction handles the initial data capture from supplier documents, achieving 90% accuracy on standard formats. Deterministic validation steps check each component against inventory levels and design specifications. Discrepancies are routed to the responsible engineer with full context, reducing resolution time from hours to minutes.

Engineers can modify extraction rules and validation logic directly as new suppliers and component types are introduced, without waiting for IT support.

## Results

BOM processing time dropped by 61%, and engineers save an average of 3.5 hours per week that previously went to manual data entry and cross-referencing. Extraction accuracy on standard supplier formats reaches 90%, with continuous improvement as the team refines rules for new document types.
